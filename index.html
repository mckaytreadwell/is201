Study Abroad: The Geography of Indonesia

This unique program allows students to explore both the physical and human geography of Indonesia, a vast island-nation. Diverse religious, ethnic, and cultural backgrounds dot the islands which we visit. Sponsored by the Geography Department at Brigham Young University, this program takes students on an unforgettable six-week journey across the country and pushes them to new heights (literally) by summiting volcanoes, backpacking, language study, and historical research. These individuals were the first ever from BYU to hike Mount Tambora, a volcano with significant history to the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints. Check out a map of the itinerary here.

Bali

Your first stop is a paradise steeped in rich cultural tradition. Take your pick from the myriad activites available, including paragliding, snorkeling with manta rays, kayaking through mangroves, canyoneering, Hindu temple visits, and much more. Watch out for the wild monkeys roaming the island.

Sumbawa

Get your dramamine ready. A flight on a propeller plane lands you in a deserted airport in Bima. Don't get comfortable yet, you have a 4 hour car ride to the base of the volcano, and then a 5 hour jeep ride up to base camp. The long journey will be well worth the sunrise at Tambora, with majestic scenery untouched by mankind in all directions.

East Java

Make sure to rest up, you'll be hiking two volcanoes back-to-back during this leg of the journey. The midnight climbs are well-worth the view. Mount Ijen flows with blue lava, formed by molten sulfur. You'll also hike Mount Bromo, a sacred Hindu sight frequented by tourists.

Yogyakarta

Explore the roots of Javanese culture in this unique city. Make world-famous Batik, take a jeep tour up Merapi, and see the remnants of centuries of colonization in Indonesia. Meet with local Muslim University Students to discuss religious tolerance and education. Take a stroll down Malioboro to see Javanese cultural icons.

Jakarta

Rich historical treasures are found in museums and site visits. Notice the diverse ethnic groups found in the capital city of this nation comprising over 17,000 islands.

Krakatoa

An early morning starts with sailing toward the open sea. Eventually a cluster of islands is visible on the horizon. Sail through to the center of the collapsed caldera toward the newly formed mound of ash and sulfur in the middle of the ancient site. As you land on the beach, notice floating pumice in the water. Hike to the summit to see the island form in real time.

Aceh

Explore the sites of the 2004 Boxing Day Tsunami, evidenced by the scars still present throughout the area. Hear heartwrenching stories of loss and hope as communities banded together to rebuild. Enjoy local Mosques, foods, and pristine scenery that also make Aceh one of the most culturally rich areas of Indonesia.

Gunung Leuser

Drop your bags at a local hotel and prepare for an otherworldy experience in Gunung Leuser National Park. Keep your eyes peeled for Orangutans and other wildlife, which make the park famous! Cool off and stay the night near the river, and raft out of the park the next day.

Medan

The last leg of your journey takes you through the North Sumatran highlands of Berastagi. Enjoy the cooler temperatures and make a pit stop at the famous Berastagi fruit market. This unique area is home to a Christian minority in Indonesia. Stop by the local markets for woven "ulos" that are unique to this area.
